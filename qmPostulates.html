Quantum Postulates

The Ontological Postulates

These define what fundamental objects in our framework are.
  1. A state is a ray (a vector with infinite magnitude) in a complex separable Hilbert space, \(\mid\vec{\psi}\rangle\).

    This indicates that each of the countable bases in the space represent some identifying physical quality, and we care more about the direction of a ray, rather than the magnitude of a vector.

  2. Any physical quantity \(\mathscr{A}\) can be represented as an observable: a self-adjoint, Hermitian operator \(A\) in Hilbert space.

    (The reverse is also true, all self-adjoint operators \(A\) represent some physical quantity \(\mathscr{A}\).)

The Measurement Postulates

  1. The result of measuring a physical quantity \(\mathscr{A}\), is some eigenvalue of the corresponding observable \(A\), \(\lambda\).

  2. Measuring \(\mathscr{A}\) and observing quantity \(\lambda\) transforms the initial state of the system \(\mid\vec{\psi}\rangle\) into \(\hat{P}_\lambda\mid\vec{\psi}\rangle\), where \(\hat{P}_\lambda\) is the projector to \(\lambda\)'s eigenspace.

    This means that that initial state is destroyed and totally lost; the only thing that remains is \(\mid\vec{\psi}\rangle\)'s shadow in that eigenspace.
  3. The probabilty of measuring \(\lambda\) is \(\frac{||\hat{P}_\lambda\mid\vec{\psi}\rangle||^2}{||\mid\vec{\psi}\rangle||^2}\). (Born's Rule)

  4. The state of an isolated system evolves as a time unitary: \(\mid\psi(t)\rangle=U(t)\mid\psi(t=0)\).

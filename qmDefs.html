Quantum Definitions

Expectation Value

The expectation value of an observable \(A\) when the system is already in state \(\mid\vec{\alpha}\rangle\) is: \[\langle A\rangle_\alpha=\langle\vec{\alpha}\mid A\mid\vec{\alpha}\rangle\]

Note: This definition matches the traditional defintion of expected value. Say that state \(\mid\vec{\alpha}\rangle\) is a combination of eigenvectors of \(A\), which include \(\mid\vec{a'}\rangle\) and \(\mid\vec{a''}\rangle\) that correspond to eigenvalues \(a'\) and \(a''\): \[ \begin{align} \langle A\rangle_\alpha &= \langle\vec{\alpha}\mid A\mid\vec{\alpha}\rangle \\ &= \Sigma_{a''}\Sigma_{a'} \langle\vec{\alpha}\mid \vec{a''}\rangle\langle\vec{a''}\mid A\mid \vec{a'}\rangle\langle\vec{a'}\mid\vec{\alpha}\rangle \\ &= \Sigma_{a'} \langle\vec{\alpha}\mid \vec{a'}\rangle\langle\vec{a'}\mid A\mid \vec{a'}\rangle\langle\vec{a'}\mid\vec{\alpha}\rangle \\ &= \Sigma_{a'} |\langle\vec{\alpha}\mid \vec{a'}\rangle|^2\langle\vec{a'}\mid A\mid \vec{a'}\rangle\\ &= \Sigma_{a'} |\langle\vec{\alpha}\mid \vec{a'}\rangle|^2 a' \\ \end{align} \] Since the double summation has terms that are 0 whenever \(a''\neq a'\), and \(\langle\vec{a'}\mid A\mid \vec{a'}\rangle=a'\), since it is the expectation value of \(A\) when the system has alredy been measured and \(a'\) observed. Clearly, if you measure \(A\) multiple times in quick succession, you will always get the same value, so \(\langle\vec{a'}\mid A\mid \vec{a'}\rangle=a'\).

Commutators

The commutator of two observables, \(A\) and \(B\), have commutator: \[[A, B]=AB-BA\] They are said to commute if \([A,B]=0\), which means that the order in which you measure \(A\) and \(B\) doesn't affect the measurements, and that the probability of each measurement is unchanged.

Anti-commutators

The anti-commutator of observables \(A\) and \(B\) is \[\{A,B\}=AB+BA\]

Compatible Observables

Two operators, \(A\) and \(B\) are compatible if: \[A=A^\dagger, B=B^\dagger, [A,B]=0\] You can define a common basis of eigenvectors for compatible operators.

Incompatible Observables

Two observables, \(A\) and \(B\) are incompatible if: \[[A,B]\neq 0\]
